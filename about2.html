About Pusat Studi Interdisipliner Agama dan Budaya (PSIAB)

Study and research in the field of religion is a field that has developed greatly in recent decades. Through this field, world religions have built their own entities so that each religion continues to develop. This then has an external impact, where religions are able to contribute to the progress of human civilization. This progress is also accompanied by the development of relations between one religion and other religions. Cooperation in inter-faith or inter-religious relations (interfaith/inter-religious relationships) has been, is, and will continue to be an important subject

In the international or global arena, the above progress then gave birth to a new discipline, namely in the field of Muslim-Christian relations or better known by the abbreviation C-MR (Christian-Muslim Relations). At several universities in the West, this field has become a new discipline of study and research, so they specifically open this department as an academic degree program at undergraduate and postgraduate levels. Also, various institutions or study and research centers in the field of Muslim-Christian relations were also established to develop this new discipline or field.




Vision and Mission

Our vision is to cultivate interdisciplinary Muslim-Christian religious relations and cultures, which are authentic, lively, and respectful, through transformed thoughts and attitudes so as to have an impact on the benefit of the nation and humanity

Our mission is to facilitate the collaboration and mutual learning between Muslim and Christian intellectuals in an atmosphere characterized by authenticity, vibrancy, and respect for interfaith and interdisciplinary relations.

Vision and Mission

Hidayah Bangsa Foundation, Salatiga, is a socio-religious foundation whose vision and mission is to build a religious, modern, and independent Islamic youth generation. The focus of the foundation is on developing and empowering the entire potential of the young generation of Islam, especially among the pesantren communities, women, and young kyais, so that they become individuals who can contribute to harmonious and peaceful relations between religious communities. In 2003, the Hidayah Bangsa Foundation also started and housed a new institution, the Center for Interdisciplinary Studies on Religion and Culture (CISRC), in Semarang City, which aims to bridge and develop inter-religious relations, especially between Muslims and Christians, based on academic study and research, so that it can contribute practically and contextually to the peace and wellfare of the whole nation of Indonesia.
