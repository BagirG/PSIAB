Peace Camps

Peace camps within local communities give an opportunity for people to meet face to face, share meals, share stories, listen to the other’s perspective, discuss topics of peace and reconciliation together, and then unite around projects for change within their community. These camps are inter-faith, inter-cultural, and international, where diversity is celebrated and first steps are taken towards better relations and healthier communities.

The camps centre on dialogue rather than input, allowing all participants to be heard and to hear others. Major topics covered within a camp are:


✔ Peace in Muslim-Christian Perspectives
✔ Diversity and Cooperation
✔ The Nature and Models of Conflict
✔ Conflict Resolution and Transformation
✔ Forgiveness and Acceptance
✔ Collaborative Peace Projects



A peace camp is intended to empower and equip local communities with all the tools they need to multiply and enhance peace and conflict transformation. Moreover, through this program, participants are equipped to become agents of peace and reconciliation, who will equip other participants for the same purpose.
